# Using ARVOS with Apple Watch

Stream extra motion data from your Apple Watch to get even more sensor readings!

## What You Get

When you pair your Apple Watch with ARVOS, you get:

- **Watch motion sensors** (accelerometer + gyroscope)
- **Dual-device tracking** (phone + watch moving together)
- **More data points** for better accuracy

## Setup (One-Time)

###1. Make sure your watch is paired

- iPhone: Open the Watch app
- Check that your watch shows as connected

### 2. Install ARVOS on your watch

- Open the Watch app on iPhone
- Scroll down to ARVOS
- Tap "Install" next to the watch icon

That's it! The apps will connect automatically.

---

## How to Use

### Turn on Watch Streaming

**Method 1: Sensor Test Screen**
1. Go to **Sensor Test** tab
2. Find "Apple Watch" toggle
3. Turn it ON
4. Watch will start sending data

**Method 2: Automatic (Stream/Record)**
- Watch sensors turn on automatically with IMU modes
- Just start streaming or recording

### Check if it's Working

On your **iPhone** screen, you'll see:
- Green "Watch Connected" status
- Watch data updating in real-time
- FPS counter showing watch sample rate

On your **Watch**, you'll see:
- "Streaming" status in green
- Sample count going up
- Hz (samples per second)

## Troubleshooting

### "Watch Not Connected"

1. **Check watch is on wrist and unlocked**
2. **Open ARVOS on both devices**:
   - iPhone first
   - Then watch
3. **Wait 5-10 seconds** for connection
4. **Restart if needed**:
   - Close both apps
   - Open iPhone app first
   - Then open watch app

### Watch Data Not Showing

- Make sure watch app is in foreground
- Check watch battery (needs 20%+)
- Turn streaming off and on again

### Slow or Laggy

- Close other apps on watch
- Lower the sample rate (50 Hz instead of 100 Hz)
- Make sure watch OS is up to date

---

## Battery Tips

**Watch sensors use battery!** Here's how to save power:

- Use 50 Hz instead of 100 Hz (plenty for most uses)
- Turn off watch streaming when you don't need it
- Close the watch app when done

---

## What Gets Recorded

When recording with watch enabled, you get:

- All your phone sensors (camera, LiDAR, GPS, etc.)
- **PLUS** watch motion data
- Everything time-synced together
- Saved in the same MCAP file

Perfect for:
- Movement tracking with two devices
- Hand/wrist motion analysis
- Extra motion data points
- Research projects

- Keep both apps in foreground
- Wait a few seconds for connection
- Restart apps if it's not working
